Common Questions

AED Vantage FAQ

How often are AEDs required to be inspected in California?
+
California Health & Safety Code requires monthly AED inspections. Both AED Vantage programs are built around that monthly cadence — ensuring you meet the requirement every month, with documentation to prove it.
What's the difference between Program 1 and Program 2?
+
In Program 1 (Self-Inspection), your team performs the monthly walk-through using our QR code system — Z3 handles all documentation, reporting, and compliance tracking. In Program 2 (On-Site Inspection), Z3 technicians come to your facility monthly and perform everything. You get the reports either way.
What happens if our AED is used in a cardiac event?
+
Both programs include post-cardiac event support. Z3 provides immediate device replacement, assists with incident documentation, and supports post-event regulatory requirements. You're not on your own if a device is ever deployed.
Does Z3 handle the medical director requirement?
+
Yes. California requires a licensed physician to serve as medical director for AED programs. Both Vantage programs include medical direction as part of the service — you don't source it separately.
Can Z3 assess our current AED program before we commit?
+
Yes. We offer a free, confidential AED program assessment — we review your device inventory, inspection history, documentation, and coverage gaps. No obligation. Most assessments take under an hour on-site.
What's your service territory?
+
Central and Southern California — Santa Maria, Bakersfield, Greater Los Angeles, Orange County, Inland Empire, San Diego, and the border region. On-site service is what we do.

Is your AED program actually ready?

Most aren't. We'll review your current program — devices, pads, batteries, documentation, coverage — and tell you straight where the gaps are.
